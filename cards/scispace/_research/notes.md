### What the role owns
The Product Owner will own the **SciSpace Agent for Research Workflows** end-to-end — defining vision, strategy, and roadmap for an AI agent that assists researchers across the full research pipeline (literature review, manuscript drafting, citation management, plagiarism checks, visualizations, grant/patent workflows). ### Core responsibilities
- Define vision, strategy, roadmap for the AI Research Agent

- Conduct product QC — both manual checks and AI-assisted testing workflows
- Design, implement, and maintain **evaluation frameworks (Evals)** for AI/LLM-powered features (reliability, accuracy, safety)
- Use AI tools and LLMs to accelerate analysis, automate repetitive checks, generate insights — while applying strong human judgment
- Work closely with engineering to reproduce issues, validate fixes, ensure high-quality releases

### Requirements (exact from JD)
- Demonstrated ability to research deeply, break down ambiguous problems, identify product and workflow pain points with clear evidence
- Experience in product QC, including manual test plans, test cases, validating fixes with engineering
- Familiarity with using AI and LLMs to support product workflows — building Evals, designing prompts, running test suites, AI-assisted QC
- Solid understanding of LLMs, generative AI concepts, and the risks/trade-offs in deploying them in user-facing products

### Seniority signal
First dedicated PM for the Agent product. The role is "first PM for a major product line" — similar leverage to a Founding PM at most startups.

## 3. Product

### Core platform
SciSpace is an AI-powered research assistant platform with the following tools:

| Tool | What it does |
|---|---|
| AI Research Agent | 150+ tools, 280M papers, full research pipeline in one interface (the main new product) |
| Copilot / Chat with PDF | Ask questions about any paper, get citation-backed answers, explain equations/tables in plain language |
| Literature Review | Search 280M+ papers, structured results with evidence extraction |
| AI Writer | Auto-complete text, suggest citations, write research content |
| Citation Generator | 9,000+ journal format styles |
| Extract Data | Upload PDFs, extract structured data |
| Find Topics | Generates answers from 285M papers with citations |
| AI Detector | Identifies AI-generated text |
| PDF to Video | Creates video abstracts from papers |
| Chrome Extension | 300K users, 4.5/5 stars — highlights text in any paper/PDF for instant explanation |
| Mobile App | Research on mobile |
| Paraphraser | 20+ preset tones |

### The Agent (the new thing — focus of the PM role)
Launched August 2025. Unified AI Co-Scientist that:
- Conducts literature reviews
- Synthesizes findings
- Drafts citation-formatted manuscripts
- Runs plagiarism checks
- Generates visualizations
- Supports patent and grant workflows

The Agent "unifies the full research pipeline in one single interface." It has a separate credits-based pricing layer on top of the standard plans.

### Product strengths (vs. competitors)
- Dedicated citation management (Elicit lacks this)
- "Chat with individual papers" — more interactive than Elicit
- 280M+ papers (broader corpus)
- 150+ integrated research tools
- Chrome extension with 300K users (embedded in researcher workflow)

### Product weaknesses (known from reviews)
- Free plan severely restricts to ~5 papers — very high friction before paywall
- Pricing ($20/mo individual) is 67% higher than Elicit ($12/mo)
- No Microsoft Word integration (Paperpal has it)
- Performance degrades with large datasets
- Uses "Standard" AI models (not premium)
- Agent credits system adds pricing complexity on top of existing plans
- Poor customer service responsiveness (per user reviews)

## 4. Pricing structure

| Plan | Price | Key limits |
|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 | ~5 paper limit, limited Copilot queries, basic features |
| Premium | $12/mo | Unlimited Copilot, unlimited summaries, advanced lit review |
| Lab / Team | $8–18/user/mo | Collaborative workspaces, team management |
| Enterprise | Custom | Institutional licensing, wire transfer, custom contracts |
| Agent Credits | Separate layer | Credits consumed per Agent task; plan details at scispace.com/credits-guide (403'd) |

**Key funnel tension:** Free plan hits a wall very quickly (5-paper limit). Users either upgrade or churn. There is no "soft upgrade path" — it's hard stop → paywall. This is likely a major activation drop-off point.

## 5. ICP (buyers and users)

### Primary ICPs

**ICP 1 — Individual PhD candidate / postdoc researcher (core user)**
- Persona: Grad student doing lit review at 2am, stuck on a paper dense with unfamiliar notation
- Need: Understand papers fast, track citations, write faster
- Discovery path: Google search → Chrome extension → SciSpace web app → hits 5-paper free limit → upgrade decision
- Friction point: $12/mo is a meaningful spend for a self-funded researcher; no trial

**ICP 2 — Research lab / small academic team (growth ICP)**
- Persona: Lab PI or postdoc managing 3-5 researchers on a shared project
- Need: Collaborative literature tracking, shared summaries, unified tooling
- Discovery path: Individual user upgrades → recommends to team → Lab plan at $8-18/user
- Friction point: No clear "invite your team" moment from individual Premium

**ICP 3 — University / research institution (enterprise ICP)**
- Persona: Head of library or research IT procurement
- Need: Institutional license, compliance, security, bulk access for students/faculty
- Discovery path: Bottom-up (faculty/student usage) → IT procurement contact
- Friction point: No visible enterprise/institution landing page, no security/compliance page

### Secondary ICPs
- Industry researchers (corporate R&D, pharma, biotech)
- Journalists and policy researchers (Saikiran's stated future expansion)

## 6. Competitive landscape

| Competitor | Strength | Pricing | Key difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| Elicit | Better data extraction, cleaner UI, broader subjects | $12/mo | SciSpace more expensive but better citations |
| Consensus | AI-synthesized answers from papers | $10-70/mo | Similar capability, less breadth |
| ResearchRabbit | Citation network visualization, now acquired by Litmaps | Free/freemium | Visual/discovery focus, no writing tools |
| Semantic Scholar | Free, citation-focused | Free | No AI writing, just discovery |
| Paperpal | Academic writing focused, Word integration | Competitive | Better Word integration |
| ChatGPT/Perplexity | General AI with research mode | $20/mo | Generalist, less academic depth |
| OpenAI Research Mode | Direct competitor to the Agent | Bundled | OpenAI's brand power is the main threat |

**The existential threat:** OpenAI launched "Research Mode" in late 2024. The race SciSpace must win: **depth of academic tooling + citation accuracy + researcher workflow integration** before OpenAI commoditizes the space. The Agent product is their response.
