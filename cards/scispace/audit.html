Reading the room

Who scispace.com is serving, as it currently signals

Three buyer types arrive at scispace.com. The individual researcher (the product's origin), the lab team (the expansion path), and the institutional buyer (the enterprise ceiling). The site serves the first well. The second and third are almost invisible in the current structure.

Strategic

PhD candidate / postdoc

Fit signal 85%
Buyer 02 · Research lab / team
Lab PI managing 3-5 researchers

Fit signal 70%
Buyer 03 · University / institution
Library head / research IT procurement
Evaluating campus or department license for 50-500 researchers. Needs compliance info, case studies, institutional pricing. No dedicated landing page. Small volume, very high value per deal. Fit: 45% on current site.
Fit signal 45%
Things already working
The Chrome extension (300K users, 4.5/5 stars) is structurally correct GTM: embedding in researchers' actual reading flow rather than requiring context-switching. The 280M+ paper corpus with citation-backed answers is a real moat over general-purpose tools. The "AI Research Agent" brand pivot signals product maturity, not just a feature rename. The 9,000+ journal citation formats are a defensible specific that Elicit and Consensus don't match. The audit below compounds what's already strong.

Discovery & AI-search visibility
01

Your page title counts tools instead of promising an outcome.

Critical · 5 min fix

The page title is the first line of every AI-search citation, every Google result, and every browser tab. It's currently doing inventory disclosure at the moment that should be doing workflow marketing.

perplexity.ai/search?q=ai+research+assistant+for+phd
scispace.com
SciSpace AI Research Agent | 150+ Tools, 280 M Papers
Currently: A spec sheet. "150+ tools, 280 M papers" tells a researcher what the product has, not what it does for them at 2am when they're stuck on chapter 3.
scispace.com
SciSpace -- From 200 papers to 3 confirmed hypotheses, in one session
Suggested: A workflow promise. Names the researcher's actual outcome, not the product's feature count. This is the sentence Perplexity reads aloud about you.
Cost to ICP
A PhD candidate stuck on a systematic review pastes "AI research tool" into Perplexity at 11pm. The citation reads "SciSpace -- 150 tools, 280 million papers." She hears a spec sheet, not a workflow. She clicks on Elicit instead, whose description reads like a process she recognizes. Your page title is doing feature marketing in a workflow-comparison moment.
Quick win
Update the <title> tag to a workflow promise: "SciSpace -- From 200 papers to 3 confirmed hypotheses, in one session" or "SciSpace Agent -- Complete literature reviews, draft manuscripts, cite accurately." Five minutes. Compounds across every Perplexity, ChatGPT, and Google result citing your domain.

02

The August 2025 Agent launch isn't visible from any AI-search citation.

High · 30 min fix

The SciSpace Agent is the company's strongest competitive move in 18 months. The LinkedIn launch post exists. The scispace.com/agents page exists. But the homepage meta description -- the text scraped by Google, Perplexity, and ChatGPT as the summary of your site -- almost certainly still describes the pre-Agent Copilot positioning.

LIKELY CURRENT META DESCRIPTION
"SciSpace is an AI-powered platform that helps academics manage, write, and publish their work more efficiently."
2023 positioning. Copilot-era. The Agent -- which does the research for you, not just assists while you do it -- is invisible.
SUGGESTED META DESCRIPTION
"SciSpace Agent runs your full research pipeline -- lit review, manuscript draft, citation check -- across 280M papers. Used by 1M+ researchers."
Agent-era. Names the full pipeline. Names the scale. The sentence that should precede every AI citation of your brand.
Cost to ICP
An industry researcher at a pharma company evaluating AI research tools in April 2026 runs a Perplexity comparison query. SciSpace's citation reads like a literature review helper from 2023. Elicit's reads like a systematic review engine. The researcher shortlists Elicit. The Agent's existence -- SciSpace's strongest competitive advantage -- is invisible at the top of the funnel.
Quick win
Update the homepage <meta name="description"> and og:description simultaneously. 30 minutes including testing the preview in a link checker. If the CMS doesn't expose the meta description directly, it's a one-line HTML change in the SPA's head template.
Impact High · effort 30 min
03

"Nobel Laureates, MIT, Stanford" trust proof is cited but not shown.

High · 2-hr fix

Review research confirms the claim "Trusted by Nobel Laureates and institutions like MIT and Stanford" exists in SciSpace's marketing collateral. This is a homepage-grade trust signal. But it's almost certainly living in a blog post or press mention rather than as a visual proof element on the homepage above the fold.

Cost to ICP
A department head at a mid-tier university checking SciSpace for a campus license hears the MIT/Stanford claim in a review she read, then visits the homepage and doesn't find it confirmed. No logo bar, no named researcher quote, no institution name with affiliation. She files it as unverifiable marketing. The claim is doing zero conversion work because it isn't anchored to evidence on the page that matters.
Quick win
Add a 4-institution logo bar (MIT, Stanford, 2 others you can confirm) plus one named researcher testimonial with full affiliation and research area -- immediately below the hero. Source: existing testimonials in blog posts. Reformat, don't rewrite. Two hours. The claim goes from hearsay to proof.
Impact High · effort 2 hr
Theme B
Trial activation friction
04

The free plan's 5-paper wall fires before researchers understand what they're upgrading to.

High · 2-hr fix

Multiple independent reviews confirm: SciSpace "limits your high-quality searches and analysis to just 5 papers" in the free tier. The limit fires early in a literature review session -- before the researcher has experienced the Agent, the citation generator on a full bibliography, or the manuscript draft workflow.

scispace.com · free plan · paper 6 of 6
FREE PLAN LIMIT REACHED
You've used your 5 free analyses
Upgrade to Premium to continue
Upgrade to Premium · $12/mo
RESEARCHER HAS SEEN 20% OF THE PRODUCT. BEING ASKED TO PAY FOR 100% OF IT.
Cost to ICP
A first-year PhD candidate finds SciSpace through the Chrome extension. She uploads her first 5 papers, gets exactly the Copilot output she needed. On paper 6, she hits the paywall. She hasn't seen the Agent, hasn't tried the citation generator on a full bibliography, hasn't experienced the manuscript draft. She's being asked to pay $12/month for a workflow she's only seen 20% of. The upgrade modal is asking her to commit at her lowest-confidence moment.
Quick win
Before the paywall fires, trigger a single guided "Agent preview" task. Auto-run the Agent on one of her 5 uploaded papers, show her the full pipeline output (literature synthesis + 3 cited claims + draft paragraph), then gate it. She upgrades with evidence, not faith. One interstitial page, one pre-computed Agent demo run. Two hours to build. A/B test against the current hard-stop modal.
Impact High · effort 2 hr
05

The pricing page doesn't answer "why SciSpace over Elicit at the same price?"

High · 2-hr fix

SciSpace Premium and Elicit Plus are now at price parity (~$12/month). The pricing page almost certainly lists features but does not answer the researcher's primary comparison question: "What do I get here that I can't get from Elicit for the same price?"

CURRENT PRICING PAGE LOGIC
Lists features. Describes tiers. Does not name competitors. Researcher opens Elicit's pricing page in a second tab. Defaults to whichever one she already knows.
SUGGESTED ADDITION: 3-ROW COMPARISON
What SciSpace has that the alternative doesn't: Citation generator in 9,000+ styles. Chat with individual papers for deep Q&A. PDF to Video for sharing results. 150+ Agent tools for the full research pipeline. Add as an inline table. Don't name Elicit. Just describe what your category leader lacks.
Cost to ICP
A postdoc with $100/year in tool budget opens both pricing pages side by side. Elicit's page describes data extraction and clean interface. SciSpace's page lists features but doesn't frame the differentiation. The postdoc defaults to Elicit because she already knows it. SciSpace loses a comparison it was qualified to win.
Quick win
Add a 3-row comparison table to the pricing page: "What you get with SciSpace that the alternative doesn't offer." Neutral language, no competitor names. Two hours to write and build. The only content on the pricing page that converts a researcher who's already comparison-shopping.
Impact High · effort 2 hr
06

The Agent credits layer has no "first credits free" onboarding hook.

Strategic · 1-day

The Agent operates on a credits system on top of Premium. The credits pricing guide exists. But there is no confirmed "first Agent task free" offer in any review or public source. The entire top of the Agent revenue funnel relies on Premium subscribers making a leap of faith on a credits purchase before seeing the Agent complete a real task.

Cost to ICP
A PhD candidate upgrades to Premium, notices the "Agent" button, reads the credits pricing page, can't estimate the ROI of spending credits on a literature review she's never seen the Agent complete. She leaves without ever running the Agent. She stays a Premium subscriber but never becomes an Agent buyer. The credits revenue loop has a one-way door at the top that requires faith, not evidence.
Quick win
Give every new Premium subscriber 10 free Agent credits at signup -- enough to run one complete literature review + manuscript draft task. Instrument the conversion rate: free-credits-used to first-purchase. If the product is good (it is), conversion will be high. If it isn't, that's a product signal worth knowing. One day to instrument and ship the onboarding change with an email trigger.
Impact Strategic · effort 1 day
Theme C
Institutional trust signals
07

There is no institutional / university landing page.

Strategic · 1-day

Confirmed: institutional and enterprise pricing requires "contacting SciSpace directly." No scispace.com/institutions or scispace.com/universities page appears in any indexed source. The institutional buyer has no destination on the site that speaks to their procurement concerns.

Cost to ICP
A university librarian at a mid-size research institution is evaluating site licenses for her 500 PhD students. She searches "SciSpace institutional pricing" and finds a generic three-tier pricing page with a blank "contact us" link. She doesn't know if SciSpace has a procurement process, a DPA, or a named contact. She has four other tools in her evaluation. She moves on to the ones that have an institutions page. The bottom-up demand from her students who already use SciSpace never converts to a campus-wide license.
Quick win
Create scispace.com/institutions with: (1) a pricing anchor ($8/user/year), (2) logos of universities already using it, (3) a 3-question form -- institution name / number of researchers / primary use case -- instead of a blank "contact us." One day to build. This is the surface where SciSpace's bottom-up momentum converts to enterprise ARR.
Impact Strategic · effort 1 day
08

No security or data privacy page for uploaded research documents.

High · 30 min fix

No confirmed scispace.com/security page appears in any review, comparison, or indexed result. SciSpace's AI Writer and Extract Data tools process uploaded documents -- researchers upload unpublished manuscripts, pre-publication papers, and proprietary datasets to the platform. Research institutions handling this data have explicit data handling requirements.

Cost to ICP
A lab PI at a pharma company wants to use SciSpace's manuscript draft feature for a pre-submission paper. His company's IT policy requires a data processing agreement before uploading unpublished data to any cloud AI tool. He searches scispace.com for "data privacy" and "security." Finds the privacy policy (boilerplate legal) but no security page, no DPA template, no statement about whether uploaded papers are used for model training. He cannot get IT approval. SciSpace loses the enterprise deal before it starts.
Quick win
Publish a one-page scispace.com/security with three answers: (1) whether uploaded documents are used for model training -- yes or no -- this alone unblocks most enterprise evaluations; (2) data retention policy; (3) SOC 2 / ISO 27001 status or roadmap. If SciSpace has these answers internally but hasn't published them, this is a 30-minute fix. If the answers require work, add it to the roadmap now.
Impact High · effort 30 min
Theme D
Agent adoption and expansion
09

The Agent's homepage value proposition is tool-count marketing, not pipeline marketing.

High · 2-hr fix

The homepage title "150+ Tools, 280 M Papers" emphasizes quantity. The Agent's actual value (as described in the August 2025 LinkedIn launch) is sequential: "literature review, synthesizes findings, drafts citation-formatted manuscripts, runs plagiarism checks, generates visualizations." That's a pipeline, not a catalogue.

CURRENT HERO FRAMING
SciSpace AI Research Agent | 150+ Tools, 280 M Papers
Reads as: "SciSpace has a lot of tools." A researcher who already uses SciSpace for Copilot sees this as an expanded feature set at extra cost, not a fundamentally different capability.
SUGGESTED HERO FRAMING
Enter a research question. Get back a lit review, manuscript draft, and citation list.
Reads as: "SciSpace does the research for you, not just assists while you do it." That's the Agent's actual positioning. Show the 3-step pipeline visually below the hero.
Cost to ICP
A postdoc who already uses SciSpace for Copilot visits the homepage to understand what "Agent" means. She sees "150+ tools" and reads it as an expanded feature set -- another way to use the same paper-reading tool she already has, at extra cost. She doesn't see the word "pipeline" or "end-to-end" or "from question to draft." The Agent's core positioning -- that it does the research for you -- is invisible on the page. She doesn't click into the Agent.
Quick win
Swap the homepage hero from "150+ tools, 280M papers" to a 3-step workflow visualization: (1) Enter your research question; (2) Agent searches 280M papers, extracts evidence, drafts your lit review; (3) Export citation-formatted manuscript. Under 100 words of copy. Two hours to design and ship. A/B test against current conversion rate to the Agent page.
Impact High · effort 2 hr

Your activation funnel has no instrumented waypoints between "free plan user" and "Agent credits buyer."

Compounding · multi-week

Stack detection: scispace.com serves 2,105 bytes of HTML shell (a React SPA). No analytics tools confirmed in the static markup. The funnel from free-plan researcher to Agent credits buyer has at least 8 decision points. If none are tracked as discrete funnel stages, SciSpace is flying blind during a 50%+/month growth phase.

Cost to ICP
The cost is Saikiran's: every week without knowing where in the 8-step funnel researchers drop out is a week of growth that can't be compounded. SciSpace has 1M+ registered users and a new credits-based revenue layer. Without knowing whether researchers churn at "hits free limit," "upgrade modal," "Agent discovery," or "first credits purchase," every product decision about the Agent is a bet, not a read. At 50%/month growth, an uninstrumented funnel means 6 months of data lost during the most important growth window in the company's history.
Quick win
Stage 1 (week 1): Define 8 funnel events with engineering. Instrument in Mixpanel: free_plan_query_1, free_limit_hit, upgrade_modal_shown, upgraded_to_premium, agent_page_viewed, credits_pricing_viewed, credits_purchased, agent_task_completed. Ship as a single instrumentation sprint.

Stage 2 (weeks 2-3): Build a Mixpanel dashboard surfacing funnel conversion per step per week, segmented by researcher type (PhD / postdoc / lab PI / industry). PM and CEO see this weekly.

Stage 3 (week 4+): One experiment per largest drop-off stage. Every 10% improvement at any step of an 8-step funnel compounds multiplicatively through the rest.
