Where sociallcapital.com is leaving
founder-buyers and proof artifacts on the table.

Reading the room

Who sociallcapital.com is serving, as it currently signals

Three buyer types arrive at sociallcapital.com. The Series-A/B founder gearing up for a big launch (your highest-fit ICP), the head of growth at a later-stage company (the hand-raiser inside a generational company), and the early-stage founder dreaming of virality (your low-fit, attention-buying-but-not-paying segment). The site speaks to all three the same way. The first two need different signals than the third.

Strategic
Buyer 01 · Series-A/B founder
Founder, 12 weeks pre-launch
Just raised. Has a launch date. Knows the pitch deck, doesn't know the launch playbook. Found you via a creator post or a peer recommendation. Wants to see proof you've done this for someone like them. Decision: a $50K-$150K engagement.
Fit signal 88%
Buyer 02 · Head of growth, later-stage
VP Marketing at a $1B+ company
Their CEO sent your link in Slack with "we should do this." Wants to see the metrics, the case studies, the integration with their existing PR / paid stack. Decision: a multi-launch annual partnership. Highest LTV but highest scrutiny.
Fit signal 74%
Buyer 03 · Early-stage dreamer
Pre-PMF founder, no launch budget
Saw your post, fascinated, can't afford a $50K engagement. Will send a "Book a demo" form fill that wastes your team's time. Should self-select OUT before that happens, but the site doesn't help them.
Fit signal 22%
Three things already working
The Tier-1 client list is genuinely impressive (Gamma, Wispr Flow, PolyAI, Airwallex, Cartesia, Deel, Superblocks). Real, verifiable, premium logos. The "distribution infrastructure for generational companies" wedge is unique and defensible. Nobody else owns this category claim. The founder voice asset is real. Vedika's combined LinkedIn + X audience approaches 350K. Ruchir adds more. The distribution moat is people, and the people are real. None of those things need touching. The findings below are about how the site fails to make that strength visible to the buyer.
A
The receipts gap
Three findings. The loudest gap on your site by a wide margin.
01

"Guaranteed viral launch" promised in the hero. Zero view-count receipt above the fold.

Critical · 5 min
EVIDENCE
Hero verbatim: "We help companies launch their products and guarantee a viral launch." Above the fold: zero view counts, zero client logos, zero specifics. The "300M views/month" stat is buried mid-page.
COST TO BUYER 01
A Series-A founder lands here from a peer recommendation already half-sold. They scroll the hero and see a claim, not a proof. The claim alone, without a number anchored to a real launch, reads like every other agency promising the moon. The conversion-killing moment is the first 2 seconds.
QUICK WIN
Add a live ticker or static badge above the hero CTA: "47.3M views generated this month" or "10M views guaranteed per launch, see the math." Pulls the receipt up to where intent peaks.
Estimated lift on hero CTA click-through +30 to +50%
02

Nine Tier-1 clients on /work. All of them are text. None of them are proof.

Critical · 4 hr
EVIDENCE
/work lists PlayerZero, Wispr Flow, PolyAI, Airwallex, Gamma, Cartesia, Deel, Superblocks, Icon. Names only. No logos. No screenshots. No embedded launch videos. No per-client view metrics. One aggregate sentence: "generated ~500 million views across our client base."
COST TO BUYER 02
A VP Marketing at a $1B company is doing due diligence with their CEO over their shoulder. They click /work expecting to see launch videos that hit 10M views, expecting per-client metrics, expecting the shape of the engagement. They get a name list. They close the tab and brief their team to find someone who'll show their work.
QUICK WIN
Embed one launch video per client with the view count visible (you have permission, you made the videos). For each client: 1 logo + 1 hero clip + 1 line of metric ("Wispr Flow launch · 12.4M views in 72 hours"). This is the highest-leverage afternoon you could spend on the site.
Estimated lift on /work-to-demo conversion +60 to +120%
03

No founder voice. No founder photo. No founder content. Anywhere.

High · 30 min
EVIDENCE
Vedika's name does not appear on the homepage. No photo. No quote. No link to her LinkedIn (~270K followers) or X (75.2K). The actual distribution moat, the founder's audience, is invisible on the marketing site.
COST TO BUYER 01
Founders buy from founders. A Series-A CEO evaluating you wants to see who they'd be working with. The reason they're considering you in the first place is probably one of Vedika's posts. Hiding her on the corporate site breaks the trust loop the post started.
QUICK WIN
Add a "Built by" section on the homepage: founder photos, one-line bios, links to their X / LinkedIn. The pitch-from-the-founder is the strongest signal a founder-buyer can receive.
Estimated lift on founder-to-founder trust qualitative +significant
B
Brand precision
Three findings. Selling distribution-as-engineering means precision must be visible.
04

Reach claim is inconsistent across surfaces. 300M on the site, ~200M on the LinkedIn page.

High · 10 min
EVIDENCE
Site verbatim: "A distribution network reaching > 300M views per month across X and LinkedIn." LinkedIn company page (per prior reads): "200M views." Two source-of-truth versions of your headline number.
COST TO BUYER 02
A VP-level buyer spending an hour evaluating you sees both. The number that anchors your entire pitch is contradicting itself across surfaces. For an agency selling precision in algorithms, the inconsistency reads as exactly the wrong signal.
QUICK WIN
Pick the bigger, defensible number. Standardize across site, LinkedIn, X, deck, JDs. Date it: "As of Q2 2026." Refresh quarterly.
Trust-signal consistency 10-min fix
05

socialcap.uk is a 301 redirect to sociallcapital.com (TWO L's). Pick one and consolidate.

Strategic · 1 hr
EVIDENCE
Vedika's email is vedika@socialcap.uk. The .uk domain redirects to sociallcapital.com. The .com has two L's, which is awkward but it's the live one. You have a vanity domain doing nothing and a canonical domain with a typo every JD propagates.
COST TO BUYER 02
Domain hygiene matters specifically when hiring a Head of Growth EU/UK. They'd land at the typo'd .com and it signals "we haven't sweated this layer yet." For a precision-engineering brand, that's a small but loud miss.
QUICK WIN
Two paths: (a) Move to socialcap.uk fully as canonical, 301 the typo'd .com to it. UK-domain matches founder email, matches "we operate globally" framing. (b) Keep sociallcapital.com, kill the .uk redirect, accept the two-L's spelling permanently. Either choice is fine, the current both-half-on state is the problem.
Domain hygiene small lift, real
06

Hire compensation visible on /careers (₹50LPA / ₹12LPA). Client pricing and ROI invisible everywhere.

High · 2 hr
EVIDENCE
/careers shows ₹50LPA CASH (Head of Growth Bengaluru) and ₹12LPA (Thinker-Writer). No client-side pricing, no engagement floor, no ROI metric beyond "500M views aggregate."
COST TO BUYER
Trust signal is inverted. Buyers see exactly what your team costs you, but nothing about what you cost them or what they get back. The number they actually want to see (per-launch-investment to per-launch-return) is missing. You don't have to publish a price sheet, but at least one anchor metric ("typical engagement: $50K, typical reach: 15M+ views, typical revenue impact: pulled from Wispr Flow case") would close the loop.
QUICK WIN
Add an ROI strip with one numeric receipt per Tier-1 client (one line, no detail): "Wispr Flow: $X engagement → $Y in pipeline within 90 days." If clients have approved any numbers publicly, surface them. If not, replace with reach numbers as proxy.
Buyer-side trust signal repair +significant for Buyer 02
C
Selectivity as positioning
Two findings. Your wedge is "we choose you." It needs to be louder.
07

Primary CTA "View work" lands buyers on a bare text list. Strongest intent moment, weakest proof page.

Critical · 1 day
EVIDENCE
Click flow: hero promises "guaranteed viral launch" → primary CTA "View work" → arrives at a 9-name text list with one aggregate metric and a "MORE SHIPPING SOON" notice. The buyer's strongest moment of intent meets your weakest proof artifact.
COST TO ALL BUYERS
"View work" is the highest-intent click on the site. The buyer who clicks it has decided to evaluate seriously. They get nothing to evaluate. Whatever conversion the rest of the page funnels toward, this CTA leaks it.
QUICK WIN
Rebuild /work as a visual case-study grid. Per client: thumbnail of launch video, title, view-count, one-line outcome. Click expands to the full case study. The 9 logos already there are the most expensive ad space in distribution-marketing. Use them.
Funnel-conversion lift on /work +significant, persistent
08

Selectivity language exists, but it's pointed inward at hires, not outward at clients.

Strategic · 4 hr
EVIDENCE
Site language verbatim: "deliberately small (under 20 people)," "meticulously-selected individuals." Both lines describe how you pick your team. There's no equivalent line about how you pick your clients. Yet the actual positioning Social Capital occupies is "we choose you, not the other way."
COST TO BUYER 03
Buyer 03 (early-stage no-budget) wastes your team's time submitting "Book a demo" forms because nothing on the site tells them they're not your fit. They self-select IN, when they should self-select OUT. Buyer 01 (the right fit) sees nothing distinguishing them from Buyer 03 and loses the "we're chosen" feeling that high-conviction clients want.
QUICK WIN
Add a "Who we work with" section: 2 sentences on what makes a fit ("Series A or later, generational ambition, founder-led story") and 2 sentences on who shouldn't apply ("we don't take SEO clients, agency referrals, or pre-PMF founders"). The second list is the power move. Selectivity gets loud, Buyer 03 self-routes to a different agency, Buyer 01 leans in.
ICP filter quality qualitative +significant
D
Capture infrastructure
Two findings. You generate hundreds of millions of views. You capture none of them.
09

No newsletter. No email capture. No X-follow CTA. No LinkedIn-follow CTA. On any page.

Compounding · 30 min
EVIDENCE
Footer site map: Home / About / Work / Careers / Book a demo. Five links. Zero capture surfaces. The site is generating 300M views/month upstream and converting 0% of interested-but-not-ready visitors into owned audience.
COST
A buyer who's curious but not ready hits the site, scrolls, leaves, and is gone. No path to come back next quarter when they ARE ready. The world's best distribution agency, capturing zero of its own distribution.
QUICK WIN
One newsletter signup in the footer: "Join 4,000 founders reading our weekly virality teardowns" (write the number once you have it). Plus one CTA bar above the footer: "Follow Vedika on X for weekly hot takes" (links to @VedikaBhaia). Total: 30 minutes. Compounds monthly.
Captured-audience compounding +monthly forever
10

No blog. No Insights. No public teardowns of past launches. Your methodology is invisible.

Compounding · ongoing
EVIDENCE
Site has no /blog, no /insights, no public case-study writeups. The methodology that drives 500M+ views aggregate is entirely opaque from the outside.
COST
Other distribution agencies hide their methodology because they can't articulate it. Social Capital probably can. Publishing one teardown per month ("how Wispr Flow's launch hit 12M views: the hook architecture, the creator network sequencing, the timing math") would attract every founder in your ICP. The network is the moat. The methodology is just content. Letting it be content captures buyers who would otherwise never find you.
QUICK WIN
One teardown per month, written by Vedika or a team member, posted to /insights and broken into 4 X-thread excerpts. Distribution costs you nothing because your network reposts it. Each teardown attracts 50K-200K views to a page that captures emails. This is the highest-leverage content investment Social Capital could make.
Content-flywheel attraction compounds quarterly
