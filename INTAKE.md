# Calling Card · Intake

The minimum I need from you to build a card. Everything else I research.

```
COMPANY URL:        ___________________________________________________
JD URL:             ___________________________________________________
FOUNDER LINKEDIN:   ___________________________________________________
CALL DATE:          ___________________________________________________
DAYS TO DELIVER:    ___________________________________________________
```

That's it. Drop these four lines in chat with me and I take it from there.

---

## What I do with those four URLs

You give me URLs. I do the work below before opening any HTML. None of this lands in the deliverable as filler — it's all checked, named, and tied to evidence.

### From COMPANY URL
- Capture full-page screenshots (homepage, about, portfolio, services, blog) via Microlink
- Extract the hero copy, CTAs, trust signals, named clients
- Sample image pixel positions for accurate audit annotations
- Curl the raw HTML to detect the marketing tech stack (Reb2b, Apollo, GA, GHL, Hotjar, etc.)
- Find sub-brands hidden in the footer site-map
- Identify ICP segments visible on the homepage (institutional / consumer / startup)
- Note things that are already strong (so the audit doesn't sound like blanket criticism)

### From JD URL
- Extract role title, scope, responsibilities, requirements
- Identify which of the four Calling Card workflows map to their daily pain
- Pull mock-data anchors (their named clients, recurring meetings, founder-coaching scope, etc.)
- Frame the role-fit narrative ("PM who builds" vs their stated needs)

### From FOUNDER LINKEDIN
- Their public POV — what they argue about their industry
- Recent posts that reveal what they care about
- Their stage of building (just-funded / scaling / operator-led)
- Personal signals (meditation, art, sport, coaching) for the Why-Me framing
- Their network — who they cite, who endorses them
- The specific phrase they'd respond to in an opener

### From CALL DATE / DAYS TO DELIVER
- Pace the build (audit + workflows can ship in 8 hours of focused time)
- Flag any rushed corners up-front

## What I do NOT need from you (please don't pre-fill it)

- I don't need their tagline copied — I'll pull it from the live site
- I don't need their named clients listed — I find them
- I don't need a list of their tech stack — I detect it
- I don't need workflow mock data drafted — I generate it from their context
- I don't need industry-overlap mapping — I match against my own role history

If you tell me these things ahead of time, you save me ~15 minutes of research. But the *output is no better*. The cleaner play is to keep the intake form to four lines and trust the system.

---

## What I will come back to you for

After research, before I write the audit, I'll send you a **7-bullet brief** for sign-off:

1. Their hero promise (verbatim)
2. The ICP I'm framing the audit for (and why)
3. The 3 strongest things on their site (so I acknowledge them)
4. The 10 audit findings in one-line form (you green-light or push back)
5. Which Calling Card workflow leads (Morning Brief / Triage / Pre-Meeting / Reflection)
6. The one thing in their JD I plan to over-deliver on
7. The 30-day roadmap shape

You read it in 90 seconds and reply "ship" or "rework finding 06". I take it from there.
